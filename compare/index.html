Provider Profiles

Honest assessments from the licensed advisers we work with.

🏥

Southern Cross

★★★★ 4.8/5

NZ's largest health insurer with over 900,000 members. Not-for-profit structure means surplus is reinvested into member benefits rather than paid to shareholders. Widest private hospital network in NZ.

Market Share: ~60%
Est.: 1964
Structure: Not-for-profit
NZ Owned: Yes ✓
Best For: Widest hospital network & trusted brand
🩺

nib

★★★ 3.9/5
⚠ Higher Premiums

ASX-listed insurer with a digital-first approach. Note: nib premiums are typically 20–30% higher than comparable policies from other providers with no meaningful coverage advantage — the company has reported losses in its NZ book in recent years.

Market Share: ~15%
Est.: 2000
Structure: ASX-Listed
NZ Owned: No
Best For: Digital service & online management
💪

AIA

★★★★ 4.6/5

Part of Asia's largest life insurance group. The AIA Vitality programme rewards healthy behaviour with premium discounts, retail offers and travel perks — making it a strong choice for health-conscious Kiwis.

Market Share: ~12%
Est.: 1919 (Global)
Structure: Listed Company
NZ Owned: No
Best For: Vitality wellness programme & premium discounts
🇳🇿

Accuro

★★★★ 4.5/5

New Zealand's only member-owned health insurer that offers an optional mental health add-on benefit — unique in the NZ market. Competitive pricing, strong adviser reputation, and genuinely NZ-owned and operated.

Market Share: ~5%
Est.: 1971
Structure: Not-for-profit Mutual
NZ Owned: Yes ✓
Best For: NZ-owned & optional mental health add-on
🛡️

Partners Life

★★★★ 4.7/5
★ Adviser Recommended

Founded in 2011, Partners Life has built a strong reputation for the most comprehensive policy wordings in the NZ market and exceptional plan flexibility. Consistently rated highest by financial advisers for policy quality and claims handling.

Market Share: ~8%
Est.: 2011
Structure: Private Company
NZ Owned: No
Best For: Most comprehensive coverage & plan flexibility
